Almost Funny

I write jokes to unwind. They’re about things in my life. Almost jokes. Almost things.

Ambiguity
People say I’m cute “in a quirky way.”
I think that means: not exactly cute, but interesting enough to confuse the eye.
Like that romantic song— You say it best when you say nothing at all .
Could mean: “There’s something rare and beautiful about you that transcends words.”
Or: “Shut up. Please shut up.”
The thing about being kind is—you also need to be specific.
The other day, I decided to give away some stuff we didn’t need anymore.
I set it all on a table in front of our house with a sign that said “Free.”
The next morning, everything was still there.
But the table was gone.
Assumptions
When my GPS said, “Go northeast,”
I was confused… but also a little flattered.
Like—wow, this intelligent machine really believes I know where north is.
Hierarchy
Power’s weird.
In my family, I fear my son.
He fears my husband.
But my husband fears me.
Learning
I’m a big fan of vicarious learning.
You don’t need to get hit by a truck to know it sucks.
But you can’t rely on it entirely.
Because while we all want to learn from others’ mistakes,
someone still has to be the other.
Unintended
I’m pretty sure the inventor of the plastic bag didn’t mean for it to last forever.
Which is exactly why it does.
