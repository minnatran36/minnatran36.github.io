Almost Funny

I write jokes to unwind. They’re about things in my life. Almost jokes about almost things.

Ambiguity
People say I’m cute “in a quirky way.”
I think that means:
not quite cute,
but interesting enough to confuse the eye.
Like that romantic song—
You say it best when you say nothing at all.
Could mean:
“There’s something strange and beautiful in you
that words can't touch.”
Or:
“Shut up. Just shut up.”
Kindness is nice.
But it needs instruction.
I put some stuff we didn’t need,
on a table out front,
marked: “Free.”
Next morning,
the stuff was untouched.
The table was gone.
Assumptions
When my GPS said, “Go northeast,”
I froze—
then felt oddly flattered.
Like—wow, this intelligent machine
really thinks I know
where north is.
Devotion
I love dogs.
They’re like cats,
just with less dignity
and stronger eye contact.
Still—
dogs know love.
If you’ll scoop warm poo for someone
every morning, no flinch—
that’s love.
Parenting is full-contact intimacy.
You give up sleep,
snacks,
your immune system.
In return, you get love—
and hand, foot, and mouth disease.
Hierarchy
Power’s strange.
I fear my son.
He fears my husband.
My husband fears me.
Learning
I’m a fan of vicarious learning.
You don’t need to get hit by a truck
to know it sucks.
But you can’t rely on it forever.
We all want to learn from others’ mistakes—
but someone still has to be the other.
Unintended
I doubt
the inventor of the plastic bag
meant for it to last forever.
Which is exactly why it does.
