About Me

I’ve come to believe that behind every algorithm, every breakthrough, there’s an underlying question: What kind of world are we building—and who is it really for?

I’m studying Computer Science, focusing on Natural Language Processing—a field where two of my deepest interests meet: how we build machines, and how we make meaning.

I didn’t begin in tech. I started in economics and behavioral science, drawn to how people think, choose, connect. But after a while, theory felt too distant. I wanted tools—something I could build with, test ideas, shape things, see what holds up in the messiness of the real world. That search eventually brought me to computer science.

Now, through NLP, I’ve found a space where language, logic, and behavior start to speak to each other. These don’t feel like separate fields to me—just different ways of asking the same questions about how we live, and how we understand one another.

It’s not just academic. I’m raising my son in a world that’s moving fast, and I often think about the future he’ll inherit. I want to help build technology that’s not only powerful, but thoughtful. Tools that don’t just work—but care.

Einstein once said, “Concern for man himself and his fate must always form the chief interest of all technical endeavors.” I keep that close. It’s the north star I try to follow.

Stay in touch — I'll reach out when I have something meaningful to share.
