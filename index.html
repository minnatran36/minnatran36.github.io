About Me

I believe that behind every algorithm and every breakthrough lies a deeper question: What kind of world are we building—and for whom?

I'm currently pursuing a Master's in Computer Science with a focus on Natural Language Processing—a field that captivates me because it lives at the intersection of machine logic and human meaning. My background spans computer science, economics, and finance. I began in economics and behavioral science, drawn to the question of how people make decisions and shape systems—but I soon realized that theory alone wasn’t enough. We need tools to anchor these ideas, to test and build. That’s what led me to computer science.

You could say I’ve come full circle: starting with the study of human behavior, extending into the technical machinery of computation, and now returning—through NLP—to a more integrated understanding of both. To me, these disciplines aren’t separate. They form a single, connected lens for understanding how we communicate, decide, and design the systems we live in.

As a young mother raising a son in a rapidly changing world, I see firsthand how the technologies we create today will shape the world he inherits. This work feels both personal and urgent. I want to help build a future that is not just technologically advanced, but also sustainable, humane, and worthy of the next generation—children like my own.

Einstein once said, "Concern for man himself and his fate must always form the chief interest of all technical endeavors." That belief guides everything I do. I want to contribute to technologies that are not just intelligent, but ethical—tools that expand access to knowledge, make decisions more transparent, and strengthen our communities. My conviction is simple: technology should extend our humanity. One cannot thrive without the other.
