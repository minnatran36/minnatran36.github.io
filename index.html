About Me

My path to Computer Science began with a life-long curiosity about people—how we think, how we make meaning, how we try to understand each other.

I started in economics and behavioral science, drawn to the patterns behind human behavior. But theory alone felt too far removed. I wanted something more hands-on—tools I could build with, explore questions through, and learn from by doing.

That search brought me to Computer Science, and eventually to Natural Language Processing. It felt like home: a place where logic meets ambiguity, where language becomes data, but still carries the weight of emotion, culture, intention. A space where you can model human behavior—but also listen to it.

Becoming a parent only sharpened that desire. I think a lot about the world my son will grow up in—not just the technologies we hand down, but the values we encode into them. I want to help build systems that stay aware of the people they serve. That leave room for contradiction, for difference. That don’t confuse efficiency with care.

I’m still learning what that work requires. But I know it’s the kind of work I wish to do.
