About Me

I believe that behind every algorithm and breakthrough lies a deeper question: What kind of world are we building—and for whom?

I’m studying Computer Science with a focus on Natural Language Processing, a field that lives at the intersection of two things I care deeply about: how we build machines, and how we make meaning.

I didn’t start in tech. My path began in economics and behavioral science, drawn to understanding how people think and make decisions. But theory alone felt too abstract. I wanted tools—to build, to test, to see ideas take shape. That search led me to computer science.

Now, through NLP, I’ve found a way to bring it all together: language, logic, behavior, and systems. These don’t feel like separate disciplines to me—they’re just different lenses on the same questions about how we live and connect.

This work is also personal. I’m raising a son in a fast-changing world, and I often think about the future he’ll inherit. I want to help shape technology that isn’t just powerful, but thoughtful—tools that care about the people they touch.

Einstein once said, “Concern for man himself and his fate must always form the chief interest of all technical endeavors.” That’s the spirit I try to carry into my work.
