About Me

I believe every technological breakthrough carries a quiet question: What kind of world are we building—and who is it for?

I’m studying Computer Science with a focus on Natural Language Processing, where my love for building systems and making meaning come together.

I started in economics and behavioral science, drawn to how people think and connect. But theory felt too far from the ground—I wanted tools I could build with, test ideas through, and see what actually works. That search led me to Computer Science.

In NLP, I found the intersection of language, logic, and behavior. To me, these aren't separate domains—they're different ways of asking the same fundamental questions: How do we live? How do we understand each other?

This isn’t academic for me. I’m raising a son in a world that’s changing fast, sometimes faster than we can make sense of. I think often about the future he’ll inherit—not just the tools we build, but the values we build into them. I want to help create technology that’s not only powerful, but thoughtful—tools that don’t just work, but care.

Einstein said, “Concern for man himself and his fate must always form the chief interest of all technical endeavors.” That’s what I return to, again and again. That’s the kind of builder I want to be.
